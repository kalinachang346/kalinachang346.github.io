How to Play Chess

Setup

Chess is played on an 8x8 board. Each player starts with 16 pieces: 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 bishops, 2 knights, and 8 pawns.

Objective

The goal is to checkmate your opponent's king — putting it in a position where it cannot escape capture.

How the Pieces Move

  • Pawn: Moves forward one square, captures diagonally.
  • Rook: Moves in straight lines — horizontally or vertically.
  • Knight: Moves in an L-shape (two squares in one direction, one in a perpendicular direction).
  • Bishop: Moves diagonally.
  • Queen: Moves any number of squares in any direction.
  • King: Moves one square in any direction.

Piece Point System

Each chess piece is assigned a point value to help evaluate trades and overall material on the board. These values are not official, but widely used by players and instructors:

  • Pawn: 1 point
  • Knight: 3 points
  • Bishop: 3 points
  • Rook: 5 points
  • Queen: 9 points
  • King: ∞ (invaluable — losing the king means losing the game)

This system helps players assess the value of exchanges. For example, trading a bishop (3 points) for a rook (5 points) is generally favorable.

Special Rules

  • Castling: A move involving the king and a rook.
  • En Passant: A special pawn capture.
  • Promotion: When a pawn reaches the far end, it can be promoted to another piece (usually a queen).

Winning the Game

You win by checkmating your opponent. Games can also end in draws.
